Today’s Concerns. Eighty percent of conventional fertilizers and pesticides used enter the aquifers during rainfall. Most pesticides applied have not been tested for long-term effects on humans, produce or the environment.

Organic residuals from liquid and raw manure have undigested proteins which possess detrimental pathogens and phytotoxins. When manure has not been sufficiently composted, it leaches ammonia, nitrates and other anaerobic volatile compounds and soluble salts which contribute to air and water pollution through run-off into the ecosystem.

Many rivers and lakes in North America are accumulating unhealthy residuals from these sources. As a result this form of pollution contributes to devastation of the ecology and Global warming.
