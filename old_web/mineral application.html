Composting add thin layers of mineral as the organic matter accumulates. Minerals help reduce odors, lock up ammonia and nitrogen by up to 85%, retain nutrients and maximize use of recycled waste.

Broadcast is the simplest way to apply mineral complexes and is appropriate for most lawns, garden and small forested areas. Scoop the minerals in your hand and gently toss them similar to sowing seeds. If you open your hand like a fan when you toss, you will cover a larger surface. Do not be overly concerned with covering the entire area evenly, as the minerals are also dispersed with watering. Custom spreaders may also be used. Be certain not to completely fill the canister of the spreader. It is important that you leave ample empty space (at least 1/3 of the canister) as the specific gravity of the minerals causes bridging. Adjustments to the agitator or spinner plate of the spreader may be necessary. Apply organic matter afterwards. Watering is also recommended or apply prior to precipitation.

Top Dressing is a mild scuffling of minerals into the soil. To do this, we recommend scratching the ground with a rake or using an aerator prior to the application. This allows the minerals to penetrate into the scratched area. Raking will also discourage weeds and help remove thatch from lawns. We recommend an application of organic matter afterwards.

Tilling introduces minerals directly to the root zone. Digging into the ground in between the rows of produce aerates the soil and allows roots immediate access to the minerals applied. For small gardens hoe minerals into the ground.

Plugs drill 4-6 ” holes into the soil (3-6 per tree or bush or space every 2-3 feet for hedges) and partially fill them with minerals. Top up the holes afterwards with organic matter generously apply to surrounding area.

 

After applying minerals saturate soil thoroughly with fresh water or apply prior to rain. Avoid Breathing Dust.When applying liquid products, such as “Nutrient Tea” or “Nature’s Nectar” use an appropriate sized clean sprayer. After diluting concentrate, use immediately or within 12 hours.
